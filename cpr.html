CPR Basics

Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) is a life-saving technique used in emergencies when someone's breathing or heartbeat has stopped. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Check the scene for safety.
  2. Check the person's responsiveness by gently shaking and shouting, "Are you okay?"
  3. If the person is unresponsive, call emergency services immediately.
  4. Check for breathing. If the person is not breathing normally, begin CPR.
  5. Push hard and fast on the center of the chest at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
  6. Give rescue breaths if you are trained to do so.
  7. Continue CPR until emergency help arrives or the person starts breathing on their own.
